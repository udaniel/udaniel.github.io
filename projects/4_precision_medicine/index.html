Precision Medicine & Clinical AI

AI-driven virtual biopsy systems for organ transplantation

Project Overview

During my PhD and early postdoctoral work at Université Paris Cité and Stanford University, I led the development of virtual biopsy systems and automated histological classification models for kidney and heart transplant recipients.

This work successfully translated complex biostatistics into clinical tools that minimize invasive procedures, improve diagnostic precision, and assess macro-level healthcare system shocks (such as pandemics) on global transplant networks.

Key Publications

Research published in high-impact journals including Nature Communications, Nature Medicine, and The Lancet Public Health.
  • Nature Communications (2024): A machine learning-driven virtual biopsy system for kidney transplant patients
  • Nature Medicine (2023): An automated histological classification system for precision diagnostics of kidney allografts
  • The Lancet Public Health (2021): COVID-19 pandemic and worldwide organ transplantation: a population-based study
